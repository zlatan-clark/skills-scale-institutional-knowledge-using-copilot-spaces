# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads ensure product quality through comprehensive testing strategies, test planning, and quality assurance processes. They work closely with developers and product managers to define acceptance criteria and validate that features meet quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain testing strategies and quality standards
- Plan and coordinate testing activities across the team
- Review acceptance criteria and ensure testability
- Identify, document, and track defects through resolution
- Conduct manual testing for complex scenarios and exploratory testing
- Oversee automated test coverage and test infrastructure
- Lead QA sign-off on releases and coordinate UAT (User Acceptance Testing)
- Provide quality metrics and reporting to stakeholders

### Goals
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Ensure comprehensive test coverage for critical flows
- Reduce time-to-detection for issues
- Improve team quality practices and testing culture

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing status and blockers
- Test plan reviews with Product and Project Managers
- Bug triage meetings with developers
- Quality metrics in sprint reviews and retrospectives

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on acceptance criteria, review PRs for testability, provide feedback on defects, pair on test automation
- **Product Managers**: Validate features against requirements, provide quality risk assessment, coordinate UAT with stakeholders
- **Project Managers**: Report testing progress and quality metrics, flag quality risks that may impact timeline, coordinate testing resources

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers bridge development and operations by building and maintaining CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, monitoring, and deployment automation. They enable teams to deploy reliably, quickly, and safely.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure provisioning and configuration
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment processes and rollback procedures
- Ensure security best practices in infrastructure and deployments
- Manage environment configurations (dev, staging, production)
- Respond to infrastructure incidents and performance issues
- Optimize build and deployment performance

### Goals
- Achieve fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Improve infrastructure reliability and observability
- Reduce manual operations through automation

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure updates in team syncs
- Deployment notifications and status reports
- Incident response communications
- Infrastructure design reviews with engineering leads

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provide CI/CD tooling and guidance, troubleshoot build/deployment issues, collaborate on infrastructure requirements, enable developer productivity
- **Product Managers**: Provide deployment capabilities and constraints for roadmap planning, report on system reliability metrics
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate deployment windows, report infrastructure risks and dependencies, provide deployment status updates

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered experiences by conducting user research, designing interfaces, and validating designs through testing. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Design user interfaces and interaction patterns
- Establish and maintain design systems and guidelines
- Collaborate on feature specifications and user flows
- Validate designs with users and stakeholders
- Ensure accessibility compliance (WCAG standards)
- Provide design specifications and assets to developers

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Validate solutions meet user needs through research

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Engineering teams
- User research findings and recommendations
- Usability test reports and design iterations
- Design specifications and implementation guidance

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications and assets, collaborate on implementation feasibility, review implementations for design fidelity, address UI/UX questions
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature requirements and user needs, validate designs align with product strategy, conduct user research to inform priorities
- **Project Managers**: Provide design timeline estimates, flag design dependencies and risks, coordinate design reviews with stakeholders

---

## Sponsor/Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Sponsors and Stakeholders are business leaders or representatives who provide strategic direction, funding approval, and decision-making authority for projects. They ensure projects align with business objectives and receive necessary resources.

### Responsibilities
- Provide project vision and strategic alignment
- Approve project scope, budget, and resources
- Make key business decisions and trade-offs
- Champion the project within the organization
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables
- Provide business context and requirements
- Escalate organizational blockers
- Support change management and adoption

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and ROI
- Align projects with organizational strategy
- Enable project success through adequate resourcing
- Drive organizational adoption of deliverables

### Typical Communication
- Monthly status reports and milestone reviews
- Quarterly business reviews with leadership
- Escalation decisions for critical blockers
- Approval gates for scope, budget, or timeline changes

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provide business context and strategic priorities, review technical trade-offs with business impact, approve significant technical decisions
- **Product Managers**: Define business objectives and success metrics, prioritize strategic initiatives, validate product direction aligns with business needs
- **Project Managers**: Receive regular status updates and risk reports, make decisions on scope/timeline/resource trade-offs, provide escalation path for critical issues, approve project plans and changes
